# Town (town.club) — competitive scan + marketing action plan
*2026-07-05. Trigger: Town co-founder Taylor Brewster signed up at feefreeordering.com. Full page-by-page scan of their marketing surface + third-party coverage.*

## Who they actually are (calibrate the fear)
- **Town Technologies Inc., Texas — a REBRAND of "BlueVerse"**, a consumer deals app (280+ Texas businesses) that pivoted to restaurant SaaS. Their terms still list support@blueverse.contact; blog titles still carry "- BlueVerse".
- 5 people (Mason Still CEO, Alec Hernandez, **Taylor Brewster**, Drew Pickens, "Pato" CTO). **No funding found** — likely bootstrapped. Taylor writes all their content.
- **$300/mo flat, demo-only funnel, no published pricing page** — and their own Owner.com compare table admits a **$500 setup fee** while blogs claim "no setup fees" (internal contradiction).
- No hard restaurant count anywhere ("hundreds"); named customers are a handful of Texas independents; storefronts live on `*.town.club` subdomains with "Powered by" footers. US/Texas-only, English-only.
- **Their site is half-finished**: FAQ sections with questions and NO answers, 4 blog posts total, stale brand references. Polished top layer, thin underneath.

**Bottom line: they are ~our stage with better copy, not a hundred-restaurant giant. The scary part is their marketing craft, and craft is copyable.**

## What they do better (steal these)
1. **Numbers as proof, everywhere.** "$2,524.67 average monthly savings" · "8.4x ROI" · "24% more direct orders in 60 days". Testimonial format = **[Owner], [Restaurant] — [dollar outcome as headline]** ("Benny Polisi, Italian Garden: +$1,200/day In Sales"), recycled on every page.
2. **Enemy-first narrative.** Hero: "More Orders. More Repeat Customers. More Profit." + "Stop losing 20–30% of your revenue to DoorDash…". Every page is organized against the delivery apps, not around software features.
3. **7 dedicated /compare/ pages naming competitor PRICES** (Owner.com $800 setup + $500/mo; Popmenu $399/mo; ChowNow's hidden 7% "Support Local Fee"; BentoBox loyalty +$199/mo). Captures buying-moment search intent.
4. **GloriaFood-shutdown ambush page** ("Gloria Food Is Shutting Down… The April 2027 Deadline" + MIGRATION SUPPORT OPEN banner + automated import). They built the landing page for a migration **we execute better** (our importer does menus + drip photo import).
5. **On-page commission math + interactive loyalty calculator** ($50 order → $12.50 lost → "$18,750/month").
6. **Concierge demo funnel** with phone number in nav + trust chips (Risk Free / 24-7 Support / White Glove Onboarding).
7. "Built by real engineers, not AI or templates" — 2026-native objection pre-empt.
8. Segment pages (Restaurant Groups, Virtual Dining Brands).

## Where we beat them (say it louder)
- **Kitchen hardware reality**: Kitchen Order App, continuous ring (Android + iOS), thermal printing, auto-accept, missed-order auto phone call. Town's marketing never mentions the kitchen at all.
- **38 languages** (they're English-only — even Texas is Spanish-first for many customers).
- **True white-label**: verified custom domains, zero platform branding (theirs: `*.town.club` + "Powered by").
- **Promotions depth** (visible/hidden, assign-to-customer, Reward Dollars, automations, sign-up bonuses) vs their basic points.
- **Reserve-then-order** (booking + pre-order + deposit in one checkout) vs plain reservations.
- **Pizza builder / half-half.** No equivalent.
- **Reseller program.** They have none.
- **Price + transparency**: we can publish honest flat pricing where they hide theirs behind a demo (and contradict themselves on setup fees). Canada/CAD/GST-correct is open field.

## ACTION PLAN (priority order)
1. **GloriaFood migration ambush page** — /gloriafood-alternative with deadline framing, "migration support open" banner, and OUR real automated menu+photo import as the hero proof. They're winning search traffic for a capability we do better. *(Biggest asymmetric win; build first.)*
2. **Upgrade the /vs engine to real compare pages with pricing tables** — add Owner.com, Popmenu, BentoBox, SpotHopper, ChowNow, DoorDash, GloriaFood, and **Town** itself; include their published/leaked prices + hidden fees; recurring honest note where a competitor is genuinely stronger (credibility trick they use).
3. **Homepage hero rework — enemy-first + numbers**: lead with the 20–30% loss, then the outcome. Add on-page commission math and an **interactive savings calculator** (orders/day × avg order × commission → monthly loss vs our flat price). We already track `savedVsUberEatsCents` per order — surface a live platform-wide "saved vs the apps" counter once volume exists (honest, auditable, beats their fabricated-precision stats).
4. **Testimonial machine**: adopt their format ([Name], [Restaurant] — [number]) from day one. First candidates: Luigi's own restaurant + Fabrizio's clients with real deltas. Never adjectives; always a number and a face. Reuse the same 3–4 everywhere.
5. **"Never miss an order" page** — the kitchen-reliability story (ring-until-accepted on any device, thermal printing, auto phone call if staff miss it, auto-accept). Nobody in the category markets this; it's our most defensible operational moat.
6. **Real FAQ with answers** — objection-first (fees? contracts? who owns the customer data? what if my internet dies? do I need new hardware? Stripe fees? migration?). Their FAQ shells are empty — completeness is an easy visible win.
7. **Trust chips + human path**: add "Risk free · Real human support · We set everything up for you" chips near every CTA; put the support phone number (once Twilio line forwards) in the nav. Keep self-serve signup (our advantage) but ADD a book-a-demo path.
8. **Segment pages**: multi-location/restaurant groups + ghost kitchens/virtual brands (we have brand inheritance + multi-menu already).
9. **Pricing transparency as a weapon**: published flat pricing page with a "what others actually charge" strip.
10. **Program gap worth considering later**: listings/citations management ("53 platforms" is their oddly-specific framing) — evaluate a listings-sync add-on or partner integration.
